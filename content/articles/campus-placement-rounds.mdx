## The typical placement process

Most Indian campus placements follow this structure, though it varies by company:

- **Round 1** — Online aptitude + coding test
- **Round 2** — Technical interview (DSA + CS fundamentals)
- **Round 3** — Technical interview (projects + practical knowledge)
- **Round 4** — HR interview (behavioral + salary negotiation)

Not every company has all four. Product companies (Google, Microsoft, Atlassian) are heavier on DSA. Service companies (TCS, Infosys, Wipro) focus more on aptitude and communication.

## Round 1 — Online Test

This is the elimination round. Usually on platforms like HackerRank, Cocubes, AMCAT, or HackerEarth.

**What it contains:**

- **Quantitative aptitude** — percentages, ratios, time-speed-distance, probability
- **Logical reasoning** — patterns, series, arrangements
- **Verbal ability** — reading comprehension, grammar, vocabulary
- **Coding** — 1 to 3 problems, usually easy to medium difficulty

**How to prepare:**
```
Aptitude: IndiaBix.com — solve 20 questions daily for 4 weeks
Coding: LeetCode easy problems — aim for 50 problems before placements
Verbal: Read tech articles daily, practice grammar on Grammarly
```

> **Real talk:** Most students fail Round 1 because they ignore aptitude. Don't make this mistake — aptitude is eliminatable and coachable.

## Round 2 — Technical Interview (DSA)

This is where CS fundamentals matter. Interviewers typically ask:

**Data Structures:**
- Arrays and strings (most common)
- Linked lists
- Stacks and queues
- Trees and binary search trees
- Hash maps

**Algorithms:**
- Sorting (know at least bubble, merge, quick sort)
- Binary search
- Recursion and basic dynamic programming

**What an actual question looks like:**
```
"Given an array of integers, find two numbers that add up to a target sum."

Input: [2, 7, 11, 15], target = 9
Output: [0, 1] (because nums[0] + nums[1] = 9)
```

Be ready to talk through your approach before coding. Interviewers care about your thinking process, not just the answer.

## Round 3 — Technical Interview (Projects)

This round catches most students off guard. They expect more DSA but instead get grilled on their resume.

**Questions you will definitely get:**

- "Tell me about your final year project"
- "What tech stack did you use and why?"
- "What was the hardest problem you faced?"
- "If you had to redo this project, what would you do differently?"

**How to prepare:**

Know your projects deeply. For every project on your resume, be ready to explain:
- What problem it solves
- How the architecture works
- What you personally built (not your teammate)
- Any performance issues and how you fixed them

> **Never put something on your resume you can't explain in detail.** Interviewers will find it.

## Round 4 — HR Interview

This round is rarely eliminatory but it matters for the offer you get.

**Common questions:**
- "Tell me about yourself" — have a 90-second pitch ready
- "Where do you see yourself in 5 years?"
- "Why do you want to join our company?"
- "Do you have any questions for us?"

**Always ask one genuine question at the end.** It shows interest. Good ones:
- "What does the onboarding process look like?"
- "What does a typical day look like for a fresher in this role?"

## The things nobody tells you

- **Shortlisting criteria** — many companies filter by CGPA (usually 6.5 or 7.0 cutoff) before you even appear. Know your college's cutoffs early.
- **Dress code** — formal for HR rounds, smart casual usually fine for technical rounds
- **Multiple offers** — in most colleges, once you accept an offer you cannot sit for other drives. Choose wisely.
- **Backlogs** — even one active backlog disqualifies you from most companies. Clear them first.
